My Journey: Building My First Tech Blog

March 25, 2026
By Jason Fritheo Unedo
3 min read

Hello, my name is Jason Fritheo Unedo, an aspiring web developer from Indonesia. In this article, I want to share my experience building my very first blog. It all started with a simple desire: to have a platform where I could share my tech knowledge with others.

As someone learning to code, the process of building this website presented its own set of challenges. I began by mastering the basics of HTML and CSS through various online tutorials. It took about two weeks of focused study to truly grasp the fundamental structure of a webpage before I felt confident enough to start my first project.

The dominant red color of this blog was a conscious choice. According to color psychology, red grabs attention and symbolizes passion and energy. I felt this perfectly matched the dynamic, ever-evolving nature of technology. Pairing it with black and white ensures strong contrast for better readability.

One of the most difficult parts of this project was creating a responsive layout. I wanted my blog to look great on every device—from desktops and tablets to smartphones. After experimenting with several approaches, I decided to use CSS Grid and Flexbox to build a flexible layout.

The coding process wasn't always smooth. I encountered many errors, especially when adjusting the UI for different screen sizes. Often, elements wouldn't sit where I wanted them or would overlap awkwardly. However, through patience and scouring developer forums for references, I eventually resolved these issues.

My favorite part of the design is the sidebar. It features a brief profile of myself alongside a list of recent articles. I also added interactive social media icons that change color when a visitor hovers over them.

Finishing this blog gave me a huge sense of satisfaction. While it may not be perfect, it represents my learning journey. I hope this platform inspires others who are looking to dive into web development. Technology should be accessible to everyone, and through this blog, I hope to help democratize tech knowledge in Indonesia.

If you’re interested in building a similar blog, don’t hesitate to start. Master the basics of HTML and CSS, then let your creativity lead the way. The internet is full of free resources to help you reach your goals. The learning process never truly ends, and building this blog is just the first step in my long journey through the world of web development.
