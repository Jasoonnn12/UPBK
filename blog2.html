Coding Tips for Dummies in 2026

April 23, 2026
By Jason Fritheo Unedo
3 min read

Building a website might sound difficult, but the process is actually quite simple. In essence, a "responsive" website is one that automatically resizes to fit any screen. Imagine it like a balloon that expands or shrinks to fit the size of the box it’s in; it will always look neat no matter where it is placed.

The first step is choosing easy-to-use tools. You don't need to learn complicated computer languages; simply use service providers like Squarespace or Wix. These tools allow you to arrange the layout just by dragging images and text with your mouse, which is very practical for anyone.

Before you start building, it's best to focus on the mobile view first. This is important because phone screens are smaller, forcing you to include only the most essential elements. If the mobile layout looks good, usually the layout on a larger computer screen will naturally follow suit.

Choose a "ready-to-use" template or design. Services like GoDaddy provide many design options that automatically adjust themselves. By using these designs, you won't have to worry about your text looking messy or overlapping when opened on different devices.

There is one crucial setting called the Viewport. This is a simple command that ensures your website always follows the screen width of the device being used. If this setting is active, your site won't look tiny like an ant when opened on a phone, making it comfortable to read.

Use a flexible sizing system, such as percentages. For example, set an image size to "100% of the screen width." This way, if the screen gets smaller, the image will shrink neatly along with it. This prevents images from being cut off or overflowing the screen boundaries when viewed on tiny devices.

Make sure the buttons on your site are large. Since human fingers are wider than a mouse cursor, small buttons are difficult to press. Provide enough space between buttons so users don't misclick. Large, clear buttons will make visitors feel comfortable while exploring your site.

Once finished, don't forget to test the site live. You can use tools like Google Search Console to check for any errors. Additionally, ask friends or family to open the site on their phones to ensure everything is running smoothly.

Lastly, remember that a good website is one that is simple and not confusing. Don't add too many unnecessary decorations, as they can make the site slow. Stick to clear text and clean images so that visitors enjoy staying on your website for a long time.
